## Introduction
{:.no-display}

The objective of this unit is to explore the business case for web accessibility. It introduces several of its factors and helps students develop their own business case.   
It also explores different case studies for accessibility in organizations.

## Learning Outcomes

Students should be able to:
* Identify access to information, including on the Web, as a human right
* Recognize the imperative of accessibility for people with disabilities
* Identify some of the factors in the business case for web accessibility
* List specific examples of broader benefits of web accessibility
* Identify the legal dimension of accessibility for some organizations

## Competences

***Learners***
:  [Basic] Basic understanding of computers and the Web.

***Instructors***
:   [Medium] Good understanding of accessibility and inclusion. Experience in the types of business benefits resulting from accessibility is advised.

### Topic: Social benefitss

The resource Social Factors in Developing a Web Accessibility Business Case for Your Organization [^1] contains some of the social factors for an accessibility business case.  
Explain how accessibility features benefit most web users: older people, people using mobile devices, people with lower digital skills, people who are not fluent in a language, etc.  
Emphasize the benefits that accessibility can bring to the business brand, especially through Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) programs.  

{% include excol.html type="middle" %}

#### Learning Outcomes

Learners should be able to:
* Recognize accessibility as an essential discipline for equal opportunity
* Explain that accessibility also benefits users without disabilities
* Explain how accessibility can enhance the brand perception and contribute to some other benefits related to CSR
* Identify accessibility as an essential requirement due to the increasing relevance of the digital world at all levels of interaction

{% comment %}

#### Duration

Approximately 30 to 45 minutes.

{% endcomment %}

#### Teaching Ideas

* Refer to the CRPD as a resource where students can find access to information as a  fundamental right of people with disabilities. Link this reference to the corresponding international, regional, and local policies that apply in your zone. 
* Discuss how accessibility solutions are also enjoyed by many people regardless of their disability. Emphasize how many solutions originally thought for accessibility have become mainstream, such as text-to-speech or speech recognition.
* Promote discussion among students about which aspects of accessibility benefit them. For instance, ask them if they use any smart assistant or a smart speaker.
* Encourage students to research for publicly available testimonials about business cases for accessibility.

#### Homework Ideas

* Ask students to research on the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities. Encourage them to share with their colleagues the conception that it offers about people with disabilities. Concentrate on their right to access to information found in article 9.
* Ask students to research how many people with disabilities might be benefited  by accessibility. Emphasize that statistics on this matter are difficult to find, so encourage them to give a picture from their local or organizational perspective.
* Ask studetns to perform a specific task with their smart asisstant built-in to their mobile phones. Point  students to general questions such as asking for the news, or how the weather is like.

### Topic: Broader Benefits of Accessibility

Focus on other benefits of accessibility such as tecnical and financial aspects. 

* Increase web use
* Reduce Development Costs
* Enable content for different configurations.
* Be prepared for Advanced Web Technologies
* Have high quality websites
* Decrease costs.

{% include excol.html type="middle" %}

#### Learning Outcomes

* Identify accessibility as an oportunity to increase usage of products and services
* Explain how overall development costs are reduced by accessibility implementation
* Identify how accessibility benefits interoperability by allowing content to be served in different configurations and settings

#### Homework Ideas

### Topic: Legal and Policy Aspects

Introduce some of the international policies relating to web accessibility, and emphasize any applicable local policies. Explain the relevance of accessibility in today's increasingly digital world, and how the inclusion of people with disabilities is a social imperative.

{% include excol.html type="middle" %}

**Tip:** Emphasize on success cases from companies, entities and organizations that have incorporated accessibility into their products and services, and underline how their social perception increases.

#### Learning Outcomes

* Understanding that access to information through all means including the web is one of the fundamental rights of people with disabilities.
* Knowledge of international accessibility standards and policies that enforce accessibility all over the world.

#### Teaching Ideas

* Engage students in a conversation about how they could improve the right to access to information for people with disabilities in their daily activities.

## Suggested Teaching Methods

**Presentational** &mdash; Provide specific examples, studies, and references that speak to the particular audience. Where possible, relate examples to personal experiences of learners; for example using mobile devices.

{% endcomment %}

## Ideas for Assessment

* Learners should be able to explain different business case arguments for digital accessibility, and broader benefits of accessibility for people in different situations and contexts.
* Learners should be able to develop potential business case arguments for their own organization, and present this to other learners as examples of how they can apply what they have learned.

## Teaching Resources

[^1]: [Social Factors in Developing a Web Accessibility Business Case for Your Organization](https://www.w3.org/WAI/business-case/archive/soc.php#social) â€” Provides guidance on addressing social factors in a business case for Web accessibility.

* <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/business-case/">The Business Case for Digital Accessibility</a> &mdash; Explains the rationale for organizations to pursue digital accessibility for people with disabilities. This includes driving innovation, enhancing brand, extending market reach, and minimizing legal risk. It includes cases studies from different organizations.
* <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/perspective-videos/">Web Accessibility Perspectives (videos)</a> &mdash; series of 10 videos, each of around 1 minute duration, that highlight different types of accessibility features and how they impact people with disabilities and other users. For example, one video introduces colors with good contrast and how it benefits many people.
* <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/older-users/">Older Users and Web Accessibility: Meeting the Needs of Ageing Web Users</a> &mdash; Explains the overlapping needs of ageing web users and web users with disabilities, and how accessibility addresses both situations. This resource refers to further background materials, including statistics on older people.
* <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag-mobile-overlap/">[Archived] Web Content Accessibility and Mobile Web: Making a Website Accessible Both for People with Disabilities and for Mobile Devices</a> &mdash; Explains some of the shared experiences using the Web for people using mobile devices and people with disabilities (archived content).
* <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/fundamentals/accessibility-usability-inclusion/">Accessibility, Usability, and Inclusion</a> &mdash; Explains the distinctions and overlaps between the terms accessibility, usability, and inclusion, and how these are best addressed together using a user-centered design process.
* <a href="https://www.w3.org/WAI/policies/">Web Accessibility Laws &amp; Policies</a> &mdash; Lists some of the laws and policies relating to web and digital accessibility internationally.
* <a href="https://www.un.org/development/desa/disabilities/convention-on-the-rights-of-persons-with-disabilities.html">Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD)</a> &mdash; Lists the countries that signed and ratified the convention and optional protocol, and provides news and information on implementation of the convention.

## Notes

* **Cultural aspects** &mdash; The cultural acceptance and approach to accessibility and disability varies between countries and even individual organizations. The business case arguments may need to be adjusted accordingly.
* **Business context** &mdash; The business context that learners are used to may also require adjustment of the business case arguments. For example, to better relate to their industry sector and organizational culture.
